Early Life

Washington was born on February 22, 1732, in Westmoreland County, Virginia, to a family of modest wealth. As a young man, he worked as a surveyor and later inherited Mount Vernon, a plantation in Virginia. His early military experience came during the French and Indian War , where he served as a British officer.

Role in the American Revolution

Washington was appointed Commander-in-Chief of the Continental Army in 1775 during the American Revolution. His leadership was instrumental in sustaining the colonial forces through hardships, including the brutal winter at Valley Forge. He achieved notable victories, such as the surprise attack at Trenton and the decisive victory at Yorktown in 1781, which secured American independence.

First President of the United States

In 1789, Washington was unanimously elected as the first President of the United States, serving two terms from 1789 to 1797. His presidency set many important precedents, including the formation of a cabinet and the peaceful transfer of power. Washington was a strong advocate for national unity and warned against political factions and foreign entanglements in his Farewell Address.

Legacy

Washington is remembered as a symbol of integrity, leadership, and patriotism. His Mount Vernon estate remains a historic site, and his image appears on the U.S. dollar bill and quarter. He died on December 14, 1799, at Mount Vernon, but his legacy as a unifying leader and foundational figure of the United States endures.
